Budgeting

The very base of personal finance is the budget. A budget can help you generate a positive, reliable cash flow, and will keep you more knowledgeable on your finances in general, as you will have to handle them at least once a month. This guide will teach you to analyze your spending, identify areas in which you can save, and then focus on cutting expenses.

Analyzing Your Spending

Before you begin writing a budget, you have to analyze your spending.

For one month, track all of your expenses. Simply tracking your spending will probably cut down your expenses as you'll see the aggregate sum you've spent every time you log another purchase. You can do this manually with a simple note-taking app, or if you want something that will track all your expenses automatically, you can use Mint.

Mint will record all transactions that occurs in the accounts you link to it, and also create graphs out of the data it collects, so you will be able to see all of your transactions and visualize your aggregate spending in one place.

After a month, look at your total expenses for every category. How much did you spend on Food? Entertainment? Now that you know, it's time to identify areas in which you can save.
